## A.2 — Full catalog, by thread

Format: **Name · year · country · kind** — why — *fills*. Confidence noted where below high.

### Chip Lithography
- **Nikon NSR Stepper · 1984 · Japan · Manufacture** — displaced US GCA to take ~half the world stepper market. *Japan's first-ever litho card; the admitted blind spot.*
- **Canon Stepper · 1980 · Japan · Manufacture** — second pillar of Japanese litho. *With Nikon, a real Japan HOLD not a single turn.* (date soft; first true Canon stepper ~1984)
- **Zeiss EUV Projection Optics · 2017 · Germany · Manufacture** — the multilayer mirrors ASML's EUV cannot image without. *Germany's only modern litho card (it holds just the 1440 press).*
- **Japanese Photoresist (Tokyo Ohka/JSR) · 1990 · Japan · Manufacture** — came to supply most of the world's advanced resist. *Deepens the Japan hold; corpus cards only the US 1982 amplified resist.* (medium)
- **Cymer Excimer Laser Source · 1986 · USA · Make** — the DUV/EUV light source every scanner runs on. *Cards a tool the essay names; lowest priority — another US card in an over-told run.*

### Precision Optics
- **Nikon Projection Lens · 1984 · Japan · Manufacture** — high-NA lenses that out-resolved the American steppers. *Japan's first optics card; the named gap.*
- **Schott Optical Glass (Jena) · 1884 · Germany · Manufacture** — reproducible optical glasses, the industrial base of modern lenses. *Fills the 84-yr Abbe(1873)→photolithography(1957) silence; 11-yr German hold with Abbe.*
- **Zeiss DUV/EUV Projection Optics · 2010 · Germany · Manufacture** — the lenses/mirror stacks that set every scanner's resolution. *Extends Germany's optics lead past its last card (Abbe 1873).*
- **Achromatic Doublet (Dollond/Hall) · 1758 · UK · Make** — crown+flint pairing that cancelled chromatic aberration. *Fills the 129-yr Leeuwenhoek(1676)→Guinand(1805) gap.* (medium)

### Seeing Smaller
- **Phase-Contrast Microscope · 1933 · Netherlands · Measure** — let unstained living cells be seen (Zernike, Nobel 1953). *Netherlands' second card, 257 yr after Leeuwenhoek; fills part of 1931→1981.*
- **Lister's Achromatic Microscope · 1830 · UK · Make** — corrected objectives that founded modern microscopy. *Breaks the 197-yr Leeuwenhoek→Abbe silence, the thread's largest.*
- **SEM (Stereoscan) · 1965 · UK · Measure** — first commercial scanning electron microscope (Oatley, Cambridge). *Fills the 50-yr 1931→1981 gap.* (medium)
- **STED Super-Resolution · 2000 · Germany · Measure** — beat Abbe's diffraction limit in light (Hell, Nobel 2014). *Newest card; closes the loop on the Abbe limit the panel opens with.* (medium; 4th German card)

### Materials Characterization
- **Raman Spectroscopy · 1928 · India · Measure** — inelastic light scattering, a fingerprint for molecular bonds (Nobel 1930). ***India's only card in the thread*** — turns total absence into a real Indian turn.

- **FT-NMR · 1966 · Switzerland · Measure** — Fourier NMR resolving molecular structure (Ernst, Nobel 1991). *1966–1981 Swiss HOLD; the central structural tool the corpus lacks.*
- **Mössbauer Spectroscopy · 1958 · Germany · Measure** — recoil-free gamma resonance (Nobel 1961). *Continues the German lead into the postwar 1952→1981 gap.* (medium)
