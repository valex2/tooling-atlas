*Manufacture · Kawasaki, Japan · 1990s*
**Shigeo Nakamura** · epoxy film pressed on in layers

**Front.** Shigeo Nakamura [joined Ajinomoto's central research laboratory in Kawasaki in 1992](https://www.ajinomoto.com/aboutus/officers/shigeo-nakamura), fresh out of a master's degree, and was put on electronic materials, a small side business grown out of what the company had learned from the leftovers of making monosodium glutamate. In 1996 [a processor maker asked whether the group could supply the insulating layer of a chip package as a film](https://www.ajinomoto.com/stories/sprinting-a-marathon-the-pioneering-technology-behind-todays-computer-brains). Board shops at the time painted liquid resin on, dried it, turned the board over, painted, dried again. Nakamura's team mixed epoxy resin, a hardener and very fine mineral powder into a varnish, then [spread it on a sheet of PET plastic and dried it into a layer of its own](https://polymerinnovationblog.com/polymers-in-electronic-packaging-build-up-films-for-flip-chip-semiconductor-substrates-part-one/), [20 to 30 micrometres thick, about a third the width of a hair](https://www.ajinomoto.co.jp/company/jp/rd/aspj/abf/). The roll goes into a press that pumps out the air first. The resin softens under [about 10 kilograms of force per square centimetre](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%91%B3%E3%81%AE%E7%B4%A0%E3%83%93%E3%83%AB%E3%83%89%E3%82%A2%E3%83%83%E3%83%97%E3%83%95%E3%82%A3%E3%83%AB%E3%83%A0) and flows down into every dip in the board under it, and it sets as a flat skin with no bubbles trapped inside. A laser then burns [holes about 50 micrometres across](https://meridian.allenpress.com/ism/article/2017/1/000113/35865/Fine-Line-Routing-and-Micro-via-Patterning-in-ABF) straight down through it, copper is plated only where the pattern wants it, and the next film goes on top. The resin Nakamura settled on had to be kept refrigerated and no laminating machine on the market would run it, so he spent his weekends at the machine builder's works until one would.

**Back.** The film shipped in 1999 on the [Pentium III](https://www.ajinomoto.com/aboutus/officers/shigeo-nakamura). Plating copper only where it belongs, rather than coating the whole face and etching most of it away, [pulled line widths from 75 micrometres down to 15](https://www.porterprize.org/english/pastwinner/2012/12/01171326.html), and packages kept adding layers until an [AI accelerator substrate carried more than twenty of them](https://www.datagravity.dev/p/the-abf-substrate-bottleneck). Five substrate makers run the film under licence, Ibiden and Shinko in Japan, Unimicron, Nan Ya and Kinsus in Taiwan. Ajinomoto still coats [more than 95 percent of the world's supply](https://wccftech.com/msg-maker-ajinomoto-to-raise-prices-as-abf-substrate-supply-demand-gap-extends-to-2027/) out of two plants, in Kawasaki and Gunma, and has [spent 25 billion yen to lift output by half](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/04/01/news-japanese-firm-ajinomoto-to-invest-jpy-25-billion-by-2030-to-expand-abf-production-for-advanced-packaging/) with a [third site bought in Gifu](https://www.ajinomoto.com/cms_wp_ajnmt_global/wp-content/uploads/pdf/2026_05_07_02E.pdf) that will not run until 2032. Sekisui Chemical started selling a rival film in 2014 and holds about five percent, because getting a new film qualified on a top substrate line takes a year and a half to two years of testing. Nakamura was made [chief executive of the whole company in 2025](https://www.ajinomoto.com/aboutus/officers/shigeo-nakamura).
