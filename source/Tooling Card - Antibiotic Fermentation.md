*Manufacture · Shijiazhuang, China · 2000s*
**North China Pharmaceutical and the Chinese fermentation plants** · grow and split the antibiotic core base

**Front.** Almost no antibiotic is the raw mold broth itself. You grow penicillin in a big tank, then an enzyme snips the side chain off to leave a bare core called [6-APA, the building block that most penicillins are assembled from](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/medicine-and-dentistry/6-aminopenicillanic-acid); a sister core called 7-ACA does the same for the cephalosporins. By 2004 the question was where that growing-and-snipping happened, and the answer stopped being America: in that year the Bristol-Myers Squibb plant in East Syracuse, New York shut its fermentation vats for good, the last big US site running the wartime deep-tank method, [once making up to 70% of the nation's penicillin](https://www.pharmaceutical-technology.com/uncategorized/bristolmyerseastsyra/). Chinese plants like [North China Pharmaceutical in Shijiazhuang, the country's largest 6-APA maker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_China_Pharmaceutical_Group), had grown vats past 200,000 liters and [dropped the price of the core so far that western plants could not match it](https://www.realclearhealth.com/articles/2026/05/12/americas_antibiotic_supplies_are_hanging_by_a_thread_1182189.html).

**Back.** The tool the US invented in the war moved to China, and the US base was gone with the Syracuse closure, leaving [no domestic penicillin fermentation at all](https://www.realclearhealth.com/articles/2026/05/12/americas_antibiotic_supplies_are_hanging_by_a_thread_1182189.html). China now holds about [90% of the world's 6-APA capacity and the majority of 7-ACA](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12992-024-01089-x), and [80% of US antibiotics trace back to Chinese fermentation](https://www.thewirechina.com/2020/04/12/is-americas-antibiotic-supply-at-risk/); even India, the big maker of finished amoxicillin, [buys nearly all its 6-APA from China](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11656612/). The Sandoz plant in Kundl, Austria is the [last large penicillin fermentation site left in the West](https://bjournal.com/the-last-line-of-defense/). The vats are capital-heavy and permit-heavy, so rebuilding the base takes years, and the lead does not come back just by wanting it.

Nuance: The "70% of the nation's penicillin" figure is the East Syracuse plant's own peak claim and is widely repeated but vendor-sourced. The headline US-import share is contested: I used the durable, better-sourced framings (China ~90% of 6-APA, ~80% of US antibiotics ultimately Chinese-fermented) rather than the loose "92% of US penicillin from China" line, which traces to thinner sources. 6-APA is the penicillin core; 7-ACA the cephalosporin core; both are fermentation-grown then enzyme-cleaved, so the card folds them as one base. Honest gap: the moat here is rebuildable in principle (it is capital and permits, not lost secret knowledge), which is exactly why the dependence reads as a policy choice rather than a hard technical wall.
