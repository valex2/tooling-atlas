*Manufacture · Linz, Austria · 1950s*
**VOEST (Linz-Donawitz)** · pure oxygen blown onto iron

**Front.** In 1952, Austria's biggest steel plant, [VÖEST in Linz](https://www.primetals.com/en/metals-magazine/the-history-of-ld-steelmaking-back-to-the-roots/), was rebuilding from wartime bombing, and its engineers could not afford the huge coal-hungry furnaces every big steelmaker used. A works manager named Theodor Suess knew that [Robert Durrer](https://mat.ethz.ch/news-and-events/staudinger-durrer-prize/robert-durrer.html), a professor in Switzerland, had refined iron in 1948 by blowing pure oxygen onto it. Suess's team hung a water-cooled pipe over a tilting pot of molten iron and blew oxygen straight down; the carbon in the iron burned as its own fuel, and a batch that took an open-hearth furnace [ten hours came out in under forty minutes](https://www.primetals.com/en/metals-magazine/the-history-of-ld-steelmaking-back-to-the-roots/). On [November 27, 1952](https://www.voestalpine.com/stahl/en/The-Steel-Division/News/60-years-of-the-Linz-Donawitz-LD-process), the first 30-ton pot tapped its steel — a plain pot and an oxygen jet doing the work of a furnace the size of a hall.

**Back.** A sister plant at [Donawitz](https://www.voestalpine.com/stahl/en/The-Steel-Division/News/60-years-of-the-Linz-Donawitz-LD-process) started up in May 1953, and the two towns gave the process its name, LD. Austria licensed the pot cheaply, so it moved fast: [Japan's Yawata works](https://www.jstage.jst.go.jp/article/isijinternational/55/1/55_36/_html/-char/ja/) ran its first one in 1957 and built the world's most modern steel industry on it, while America's giants kept feeding their paid-for open hearths and [U.S. Steel held out until 1964](https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/history/voest-develops-basic-oxygen-process-steelmaking). VOEST stayed a mid-sized maker; the pot it built now makes about [seven of every ten tons](https://business.columbia.edu/insights/climate/cki/steel/overview) of steel on earth.
