*Manufacture · Wuxi, China · 2020s*
**Wuxi Lead Intelligent Equipment** · the integrated cell production line

**Front.** By 2024 the firm [Wuxi Lead Intelligent](https://www.leadintelligent.com/en/company-profile/) in Wuxi, China sold the whole machine line that turns electrode paste into a finished battery cell, end to end. A cell starts as wet paste and ends as a sealed can that holds charge. Getting there is a chain of machines that must hand a wafer-thin electrode off without a wrinkle: coat the metal foil with paste, dry it, slit it, then a winding or stacking machine rolls or layers the anode and cathode together so thin that a misalignment of a fraction of a millimeter ruins the cell. Then fill it with electrolyte and run formation, the first slow charge that wakes the chemistry up. The hardest step is that stacking machine, and Wuxi Lead's placed a layer every [0.075 seconds](https://www.batterytechonline.com/battery-manufacturing/wuxi-lead-s-wang-on-driving-battery-gigafactory-innovation-sustainability) and held [more than 60 percent](https://www.batterytechonline.com/battery-manufacturing/wuxi-lead-s-wang-on-driving-battery-gigafactory-innovation-sustainability) of that segment worldwide. One supplier now sold the entire line as a single product, so a gigafactory could be bought off the shelf rather than built from parts.

**Back.** Wuxi Lead booked a [$4.3 billion](https://www.yicaiglobal.com/news/lithium-battery-gear-supplier-wuxi-lead-to-reap-usd43-billion-from-catl-deal) three-year framework with CATL and held about [15.5 percent](https://www.marketscreener.com/news/wuxi-lead-intelligent-equipment-raises-over-630-million-in-hong-kong-listing-ce7e5adcd188f620) of the global equipment market by 2024 revenue, but the telling part is who else it shipped to: Tesla, LG, Panasonic, and a [20-gigawatt-hour line](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/american-battery-factory-partners-with-lead-intelligent-equipment-for-next-phase-of-arizona-gigafactory-302091005.html) sold to a startup gigafactory in Tucson, Arizona. The machine travels freely, so it is not the moat. Sweden's Northvolt bought the same kind of line, [burned through roughly $15 billion](https://kr-asia.com/northvolts-fall-how-hubris-and-blunders-shattered-europes-battery-ambitions), and flew in [Chinese technicians week after week](https://thedeepdive.ca/did-china-sabotage-northvolt-unraveling-the-crisis-behind-swedens-battery-giant/) to nurse the machines. It still took years to reach a yield Chinese rivals claimed to hit in [four months](https://kr-asia.com/northvolts-fall-how-hubris-and-blunders-shattered-europes-battery-ambitions), then collapsed. This is the chip story again, the same split the [[Tooling Card - The High-Yield Process Recipe]] names: the hardware exports, the know-how to ramp it to yield does not. From [November 2025](https://www.ess-news.com/2025/10/13/china-imposes-export-controls-on-lithium-ion-battery-technology/) China began licensing the cathode-process and high-density LFP technology underneath to lock the one layer it controls.
