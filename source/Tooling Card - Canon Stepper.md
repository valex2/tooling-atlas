*Manufacture · Tokyo, Japan · 1980s*
**Canon Inc.** · step-and-repeat chip pattern printer

**Front.** In 1983 the camera maker [Canon](https://global.canon/en/news/2020/20200115.html) was Japan's old hand at chip printing: it had sold the country's first chip-printing machine in 1970, and its aligners, machines that flash the circuit pattern onto the whole silicon wafer in one go, held [80 percent of the aligner market](https://www.rieti.go.jp/jp/publications/dp/03e003.pdf). But chipmakers were switching to the stepper, a machine that prints one small patch of the wafer in sharp focus and then steps to the next, and Canon kept missing it: a printer it built in [1975](https://global.canon/en/news/2020/20200115.html) drew lines finer than a thousandth of a millimetre yet never became a product, and Japan's national chip project [handed its stepper work to Nikon](https://www.asianometry.com/p/how-japan-won-the-lithography-industry). So Canon's engineers ground their own reduction lens and mounted it over a stage that walked the wafer along beneath a mercury lamp in small, exact steps. Canon [announced the machine, the FPA-1500FA, in 1983](https://global.canon/en/intellectual-property/history/industrial.html) and [shipped it in 1984](https://www.lithoguru.com/scientist/litho_history/Kato_Litho_History.pdf), six years behind GCA and four behind Nikon, but with thirteen years of chipmaker customers already on its books.

**Back.** Japan's chipmakers, then selling [nine of every ten of the world's newest memory chips](https://www.rieti.go.jp/jp/publications/dp/03e003.pdf), bought the machine at home, and by 1989 Japanese firms built [75 percent of the world's steppers](https://www.rieti.go.jp/jp/publications/dp/03e003.pdf). Nikon and Canon held [70 to 75 percent of the market between them](https://www.rieti.go.jp/jp/publications/dp/03e003.pdf) into the mid-1990s, Nikon first, Canon second. GCA, which had [90 percent of the stepper market in 1980](https://www.rieti.go.jp/jp/publications/dp/03e003.pdf), found no buyer, and its owner [shut it in May 1993](https://www.fundinguniverse.com/company-histories/general-signal-corporation-history/); Nikon and Canon were left holding [about 80 percent](https://www.fundinguniverse.com/company-histories/general-signal-corporation-history/) of the machines every chip factory printed with.
