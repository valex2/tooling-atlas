*Measure · Toronto, Canada · 1950s*
**Oliver Smithies** · molecules sorted by size in a jelly

**Front.** To study the molecules of life you first have to pull a soupy mix apart into its separate pieces, and in the early 1950s the ways to do that were crude and blurry. In 1955 [Oliver Smithies](https://ohhgp.semel.ucla.edu/people/oliver-smithies/), then in Toronto, poured a slab of [cooked potato starch into a gel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gel_electrophoresis) and ran an electric current across it. Charged molecules crawled through the gel's tangle of pores, and the small ones slipped through faster and travelled farther, so a mixture spread itself out into a ladder of sharp bands by size. The lucky part was the precision: the pores were close to the size of the molecules themselves, so even near-twins split apart. The new thing was a cheap jelly that sorts molecules by size, by eye, on a bench.

**Back.** Swapped to a seaweed jelly called agarose in the [1970s, it became the standard way to handle DNA](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4846332/), and from there the single most-used tool in all of molecular biology. You read a [sequencing ladder off a gel](https://www.thermofisher.com/us/en/home/life-science/cloning/cloning-learning-center/invitrogen-school-of-molecular-biology/na-electrophoresis-education/na-separation-overview.html), you check that a PCR worked by looking for its band, you size the fragments that restriction enzymes cut. It is the quiet step under sequencing, cloning, and PCR alike, run the same simple way in every lab on Earth seventy years on.

A no-moat Make-and-Measure tool: cheap, published, universal, the kind of plumbing that decides whether a field can move fast. **Nuance.** Arne Tiselius pioneered electrophoresis without a gel (moving-boundary, 1937, Nobel 1948); Smithies' 1955 starch gel is the practical gel form, and agarose for DNA is a 1970s adaptation, all folded here into the one tool. The card dates to the gel's invention rather than its DNA heyday to keep the lineage honest.
