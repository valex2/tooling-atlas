*Manufacture · Japan · 1990s*
**Tokyo Ohka Kogyo / JSR** · ultra-pure film for printing chips

**Front.** In 1990 the [three biggest chipmakers in the world](https://www.chipestimate.com/IC-Insights-Bulletin-Tracking-the-Top-10-Semiconductor-Sales-Leaders-Over-26-Years/Mentor/news/15254) were all Japanese, and a chemical company in Kawasaki named [Tokyo Ohka Kogyo](https://www.tok.co.jp/eng/who-we-are/history) had sold them photoresist, the light-sensitive liquid a fab paints on every wafer, since 1968. Those customers now wanted to print with a shorter light, and the amplified film from IBM's labs that could take it was famously touchy: [a whiff of amine in the fab's air](https://www.sciencehistory.org/stories/magazine/patterning-the-world-the-rise-of-chemically-amplified-photoresists/) or a trace of metal in the bottle spoiled the print. In the [latter half of 1990](https://www.tok-pr.com/en/products/photoresist/krf.html) TOK's chemists set out to brew that film for sale, washing the polymer over and over and filtering the finished liquid until the metals left in it measured a few parts per billion, so each drum came out the same as the last. By [1997](https://www.tok.co.jp/eng/who-we-are/history) the KrF film they brewed this way was the standard in fabs worldwide.

**Back.** JSR, Shin-Etsu and Fujifilm learned the same craft, and together with TOK the four came to brew [three-quarters](https://asiatimes.com/2021/09/a-big-deal-for-the-future-miniaturization-of-chips/) of the world's high-end photoresist. When Tokyo made every drum bound for South Korea [wait on an export license](https://www.rieti.go.jp/en/columns/v01_0201.html) in July 2019, the workaround Samsung found was a plant in Leuven, Belgium, and that plant is [part-owned by JSR](https://www.electronicsweekly.com/news/business/imec-jv-said-supplying-photoresist-samsung-2019-08/). The lead never left home: in 2024 a fund owned by the Japanese government paid [909 billion yen](https://www.jiccapital.co.jp/en/news/.assets/E_20240417_JIC_JICC_PressRelease.pdf), about $6.4 billion, for JSR, the [biggest resist maker of all](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20240419PD204/jsr-japan-ic-manufacturing-delisting.html), and took it off the stock market.
