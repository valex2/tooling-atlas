*Manufacture · Osaka, Japan · 1980s*
**Yushin Kataoka and colleagues at Sumitomo Chemical** · a column of beads that grabs gallium

**Front.** Every alumina refinery in the world was already dissolving gallium and throwing it away. Bauxite carries the metal at [20 to 80 parts per million](https://icsoba.org/assets/files/publications/2022/Papers/AA30%20-%20Selective%20Extraction%20of%20Gallium%20from%20Bayer%20Liquor%20with%20Ion-Exchange%20Resin.pdf), hot caustic soda strips about seventy percent of it out of the rock along with the aluminium, and because the same soda is pumped round the plant for years the gallium builds up in it to [100 to 300 milligrams a litre](https://icsoba.org/assets/files/publications/2022/Papers/AA30%20-%20Selective%20Extraction%20of%20Gallium%20from%20Bayer%20Liquor%20with%20Ion-Exchange%20Resin.pdf). Getting it out was the problem. You could hang a mercury cathode in the liquor and pull the gallium down as an amalgam, but [mercury leaked into the circuit](https://patents.google.com/patent/US4468374A/en) and poisoned the plant, or you could bubble carbon dioxide through and drop the gallium out with a bit of alumina, which [ate the caustic soda](https://patents.google.com/patent/US4468374A/en) the refinery lives on. Gallium is a rounding error next to the alumina, so any cure that damaged the main product was not worth running. In September 1981 [Yushin Kataoka and three colleagues at Sumitomo Chemical](https://patents.google.com/patent/US4468374A/en) filed on a different approach: make the grabbing happen on a solid instead of in the liquid. They started from beads of acrylonitrile plastic and treated them with hydroxylamine, which leaves each bead studded with amidoxime groups, a nitrogen carrying an -OH sitting next to an -NH2. That pair folds around a gallium ion and holds it. Aluminium, sitting in the same liquor at something like a thousand times the concentration, walks past. Pack the beads in a column, run the refinery's spent liquor through at about 55 °C, wash the loaded bed with acid, and the liquor goes back to the plant unchanged.

**Back.** The bed became the standard way to make the metal. [Ion exchange is now the main industrial route](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0304386X1200134X), and about [90 percent of the world's primary gallium](https://icsoba.org/assets/files/publications/2022/Papers/AA30%20-%20Selective%20Extraction%20of%20Gallium%20from%20Bayer%20Liquor%20with%20Ion-Exchange%20Resin.pdf) is scavenged from refinery liquor this way, at roughly ten milligrams of gallium per gram of resin and about half the gallium in each pass. Nobody has to invent the beads any more; [Purolite sells the amidoxime resin off the shelf](https://www.purolite.com/index/core-technologies/industry/hydrometallurgy/Gallium) and any refinery can plumb a column onto its spent-liquor line. That is exactly what China's alumina plants did, one bed at a time, and China now makes about [99 percent of the world's low-purity gallium](https://www.usgs.gov/centers/national-minerals-information-center/gallium-statistics-and-information) and put it under [export licence in August 2023](https://www.csis.org/analysis/beyond-rare-earths-chinas-growing-threat-gallium-supply-chains). The West had tried the liquid route instead and lost money on it: Rhône-Poulenc built a [50-tonne gallium plant at Alcoa's Pinjarra refinery](https://search.worldcat.org/title/219935784) in Western Australia in 1989 on liquid-liquid extraction, and it is gone. Rio Tinto pulled its [first gallium in May 2025](https://www.riotinto.com/en/news/releases/2025/rio-tinto-extracts-first-gallium-from-its-alumina-refining-process-with-partner-indium-corporation) from Quebec liquor and plans 3.5 tonnes a year at a demonstration plant, and [Metlen in Greece has made 5 kilograms](https://www.mining.com/web/greeces-metlen-sees-gallium-production-at-50t-in-2028-enough-to-cover-eu-needs/) and is aiming at 50 tonnes by 2028. Purolite will ship the beads to anyone. The refineries to hang them on were sold or shut, and no bed runs without one.
