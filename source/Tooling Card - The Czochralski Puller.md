*Manufacture · Berlin, Germany · 1910s*
**Jan Czochralski, Gordon Teal** · a crystal puller that grows one silicon crystal

**Front.** In 1916 a Polish chemist, [Jan Czochralski](https://www.iucr.org/news/newsletter/volume-28/number-3/who-was-jan-czochralski), was taking notes at a desk in Berlin with a pot of melted tin near his pen. He meant to dip the pen in his inkwell and [dipped it in the hot tin instead](https://spectrum.ieee.org/modern-civilization-relies-on-this-crystalgrowing-method). When he pulled it out, a thin thread of metal hung from the nib, and it was [one single crystal, the atoms all lined up the same way](https://janczochralski.com/en/method/). He swapped the nib for a fine glass tube and learned to grow the thread on purpose: touch a tiny seed crystal to the top of the melt, then pull it up slow and steady so the metal freezes onto the seed and keeps the same pattern, one [thread about a millimetre across and up to 1.5 metres long](https://ethw.org/Milestones:Czochralski_Process,_1916).

**Back.** The trick sat mostly unused for thirty years until [Gordon Teal at Bell Labs](https://www.computerhistory.org/siliconengine/first-grown-junction-transistors-fabricated/) saw that a transistor needs near-perfect material, and that a pulled single crystal could supply it. He grew the first [single crystal of germanium in 1948 and of silicon by February 1951](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gordon_Kidd_Teal), dipping a seed into the melt and drawing it up while it turned. He carried the method to Texas Instruments in 1952, and from there it became how the whole industry makes its raw silicon. Today the machine grows a [crystal log 300 millimetres thick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wafer_(electronics)) and so clean that [fewer than one atom in a billion is a stray](https://waferpro.com/the-czochralski-process-how-waferpro-produces-high-quality-silicon-wafers/), and [about 90% of all semiconductors](https://spectrum.ieee.org/modern-civilization-relies-on-this-crystalgrowing-method) start as one of these pulled crystals.
