*Manufacture · Osaka, Japan · 1990s*
**Isao Ueoka and colleagues at Sumitomo Electric and A.L.M.T.** · a steel wire with diamond grains locked to its outside

**Front.** On 14 February 1997 eight engineers at two Japanese hard-materials companies, Sumitomo Electric and A.L.M.T., [filed a patent on a better way to saw silicon](https://patents.google.com/patent/US6070570A/en). The way it was done then was to drag a bare steel wire back and forth through the crystal while pumping in oil full of loose silicon-carbide grit. The grit did the cutting by rolling and chipping under the wire, which was slow, left the block filthy, and took a long wash afterwards to clean up. Ueoka and his colleagues fixed the abrasive to the wire instead: a high-strength core with diamond grains held in a bond layer around it, each grain about two-thirds as tall as the layer was thick, so the sharp points stood out and stayed put. The abrasive now rode with the wire and cut on every pass. Other Japanese firms plated the grains on with nickel rather than gluing them, and that version is the one the world ended up buying.

**Back.** Wound through a set of grooved rollers, one such wire makes a web of hundreds of parallel lines and slices a whole ingot at once, [four to five times faster](https://themoneycorner.substack.com/p/a-deeper-dive-on-longis-wafer-advantage) than the slurry method and turning less of the crystal into dust. In March 2015 LONGi's chief executive Li Zhenguo said diamond wire had gone [from 60% of mono wafer production to 95% in a year](https://www.pv-magazine.com/2015/03/16/ceo-interview-longi-forecasts-rapid-mono-wafer-cost-reduction_100018613/), that sawing a wafer had fallen from 50 cents to 25, and that his lines ran on Japanese wire and Japanese machines. That last part did not hold. Yangling Metron started plating wire in Shaanxi that same year and went [from a fifth of the world market in 2017 to half of it by 2021](https://theasymmetric.substack.com/p/european-solar-eclipsed-industrial-stakes), the price of the wire fell from about 1,000 yuan a kilometre to under 50, and the Japanese suppliers were gone from the trade inside five years. The wire is now a [33-micrometre tungsten thread](https://www.infolink-group.com/energy-article/quest-for-thinner-wafers-and-wires-ramp-up-tungsten-diamond-wire-application), thinner than a human hair, and of the [804 gigawatts of solar wafer sliced in 2024, 97% was sliced in China](https://www.pv-magazine.com/2025/10/17/thirty-five-countries-now-operate-gw-scale-annual-pv-markets/).
