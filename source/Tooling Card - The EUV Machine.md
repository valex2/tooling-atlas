*Manufacture · Veldhoven, Netherlands · 2019*
**ASML** · an EUV lithography machine

**Front.** To draw a smaller chip you print it with shorter light, and by the 2010s the next step needed light so short, [13.5 nanometres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extreme_ultraviolet_lithography), that air swallows it, glass swallows it, even an ordinary mirror swallows it. By the rules of optics you cannot build a machine that prints with it. The idea was floated [in 1986](https://spie.org/news/photonics-focus/mayjune-2023/developing-extreme-ultraviolet-lithography), the first chips did not come off an [EUV machine until 2019](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/7_nm_process), and after thirty years and billions of dollars [only ASML](https://www.asml.com/en/products/euv-lithography-systems), in the Netherlands, could build one. Its machine makes its own light, [dripping molten tin and hitting each falling bead with a laser fifty thousand times a second](https://www.asml.com/en/technology/lithography-principles/light-and-lasers) to flash a [plasma hotter than the surface of the Sun](https://www.zeiss.com/semiconductor-manufacturing-technology/inspiring-technology/euv-lithography.html), then steers it off [mirrors so smooth and so exactly placed](https://www.asml.com/en/technology/lithography-principles/lenses-and-mirrors) that each printed layer lands on the one beneath to within [a few silicon atoms](https://www.universitywafer.com/lattice-constant.html).

**Back.** Today every advanced chip in the world, in phones, data centers, and AI, is printed by these machines, and each one costs [about $380 million](https://www.techpowerup.com/319071/asml-high-na-euv-twinscan-exe-machines-cost-usd-380-million-10-20-units-already-booked). Nothing about how it works is secret: the physics has been public since 1986, much of the research [paid for by the US government](https://newsreleases.sandia.gov/partners-unveil-first-extreme-ultraviolet-chip-making-machine/), and still only ASML can build one, while [Nikon and Canon, who once split the lithography market, never got there](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extreme_ultraviolet_lithography). The blueprint is free and the machine still cannot be copied, because the moat is the craft of holding a position to a fraction of an atom on every wafer all day. That is why [China, unable to buy one, prints its best chips with older, longer light](https://www.scmp.com/tech/tech-war/article/3278535/china-hit-hard-new-dutch-export-controls-asml-chip-making-equipment), while the lead sits in the [[Tooling Card - Leeuwenhoek's Microscope|same Dutch low country that first ground glass to see the unseen]].

Fragility has a flip side worth a framework line, that tacit craft institutionalized across an ecosystem becomes both unbuyable and unkillable. **Nuance.** The $380M figure is the next-generation High-NA EXE machine; first-generation EUV (NXE) runs nearer $150–200M. "First chips 2019" is TSMC's N7+ in high-volume production (Samsung close behind). "Hotter than the Sun's surface" is sourced to Zeiss; tin-plasma temperatures run far above the Sun's ~5,800 K surface. Mirrors are ~40 molybdenum-silicon layers at ~70% reflectivity each, so only a few percent of the light survives ~10 bounces. ASML integrates; the optics are Zeiss, the light source Cymer (now ASML-owned).
