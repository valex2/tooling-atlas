*Make · Los Alamos, New Mexico, USA · 1940s*
**George Kistiakowsky** · shaped explosives that bend a blast inward

**Front.** By summer 1944 Los Alamos had a problem it could not shoot its way out of. The simple plan was a gun: fire one slug of fissile metal into another to make a critical mass. That worked for uranium, but [plutonium spontaneously fissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fat_Man) too fast, so a gun would fizzle before it ever went off. The only way left was to crush a ball of plutonium from every side at once, and an ordinary explosive shell pushes *outward*, never cleanly in. [George Kistiakowsky](https://www.nps.gov/people/manhattan-project-scientists-george-kistiakowsky.htm)'s explosives group built the fix: a block of two explosives, a [fast one (Composition B) wrapped around a slow one (Baratol)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fat_Man). Machined to exact 3D shapes, the fast wave outruns the slow one, so the ragged outward blast bends into one smooth shell heading inward. [Thirty-two of these lenses](https://www.nps.gov/articles/000/the-gadget.htm) fit together like a soccer ball. Fired [within ten-billionths of a second of each other](https://lflank.wordpress.com/2015/01/14/fat-man-and-little-boy-the-design-of-the-atomic-bombs/), they squeezed the core to [about twice its normal density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fat_Man) in a few millionths of a second.

**Back.** The shaped lens was the hard, non-obvious part: the gun was easy, the symmetric squeeze was the tool nobody had. Trinity proved it on [16 July 1945](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fat_Man), and the same design fell on Nagasaki. This making-knowledge was the single most-guarded secret of the war, and the guard failed, because it was the kind of secret a person can carry in his head. [Klaus Fuchs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Klaus_Fuchs) and [Ted Hall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theodore_Hall) both worked on implosion and both passed the lens design to Soviet handlers; the USSR's [first device in 1949 came out a near-copy of Fat Man](https://nuclearweaponarchive.org/Nwfaq/Nfaq8.html), down to its outer shape, four years after Trinity. [Western estimates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_atomic_bomb_project) reckoned the stolen design shaved 18 months to 2 years off the Soviet timeline.
