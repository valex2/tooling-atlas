*Make · Hawthorne, California · 2010s*
**SpaceX** · a rocket booster that flies home and lands

**Front.** For sixty years every rocket worked the same way. You burned the big bottom stage to get up to speed, then dropped it in the sea and it was gone. [SpaceX](https://www.space.com/31420-spacex-rocket-landing-success.html), a young rocket company in Hawthorne, California, thought that was insane. It was like throwing away the plane after one flight. On the night of [December 21, 2015](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falcon_9_flight_20), a [Falcon 9](https://www.space.com/31420-spacex-rocket-landing-success.html) lifted eleven small satellites toward orbit, and then the bottom stage did something no rocket had ever done. It turned around in the dark, fell back toward the coast, lit its engines again to slow itself, and set itself down upright on a pad near the launch site. It stood there on its legs, whole. The trip up was old news; flying the booster home so you could [use it again](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falcon_9_first-stage_landing_tests) was the thing no one had done.

**Back.** Once you can land a booster, you can clean it up and fly it again, and again. One single booster has now flown more than [thirty times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Falcon_9_first-stage_boosters). That dropped the price of carrying a kilogram to orbit from the Space Shuttle's [tens of thousands of dollars](https://theconversation.com/how-spacex-lowered-costs-and-reduced-barriers-to-space-112586) down toward a few thousand, roughly twenty times cheaper. The real tool underneath was the way they worked: build a thing fast, fly it, let it blow up, learn from the wreck, build a better one, and make the engines [in their own factory](https://theconversation.com/how-spacex-lowered-costs-and-reduced-barriers-to-space-112586). In [May 2020](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-astronauts-launch-from-america-in-historic-test-flight-of-spacex-crew-dragon/) the same company carried two NASA astronauts up from American soil, the first time since 2011 the country had not been buying seats from Russia. By the mid-2020s SpaceX, a firm founded in 2002, was launching more mass to orbit each year than every other rocket on Earth combined.
