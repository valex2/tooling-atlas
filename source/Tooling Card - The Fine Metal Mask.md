*Manufacture · Mihara, Japan · 2010s*
**Chikao Ikenaga** · an etched invar foil with millions of funnel-shaped holes

**Front.** In February 2015, Chikao Ikenaga and four colleagues at [Dai Nippon Printing](https://patents.google.com/patent/EP3257964B1/en) filed a patent about a film of rust. Their employer is a printing company that has been [eating holes in steel sheet since 1958](https://www.global.dnp/en/our-business/innovation/photo-mask/), when it began making the perforated masks inside colour television tubes, and by 2015 phone makers wanted screens at 450 pixels to the inch. At that pitch the holes came out ragged. The mask they were cutting is a foil of [invar](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acsomega.6c02586), an iron alloy with about 36 percent nickel that barely swells when it gets hot, rolled to [85 microns or less](https://patents.google.com/patent/EP3257964B1/en) and often nearer 20. You coat the foil in resist, print the pattern on it with light, then let acid eat in from both faces until the two pits meet in a hole shaped like a funnel, its wide mouth pointing down at the pot the material boils out of. Ikenaga's team traced the ragged edges to the annealing step: cooked in ordinary air, the rolled foil grew a skin of nickel hydroxide that the resist would not grip. They annealed it in inert gas instead, held the nickel-to-iron oxide ratio [below 0.4](https://patents.google.com/patent/EP3257964B1/en), and the resist held its narrow lines.

**Back.** In a panel plant the finished foil is stretched, welded onto a steel frame and clamped flat against the back of a glass sheet by magnets, and red, then green, then blue emitter is evaporated up through it, one colour per pass. Samsung Display took [52 percent of the smartphone panel market in late 2025](https://www.techinsights.com/blog/analysis-smartphone-display-panel-market-share-q4-2025) on lines that work this way, and BOE, LG and Visionox all pattern the same way. The etched masks come from DNP and Toppan in Japan, the invar sheet under them from [Proterial, the old Hitachi Metals](https://www.hdinresearch.com/reports/160808), and the evaporator itself from Canon Tokki, so the leader in the panels holds none of the three. Samsung Display [went looking for Korean mask suppliers in 2019](https://english.etnews.com/20190723200001), during the Japanese export controls, and backed off on reports that DNP would cut its allocation. DNP opened a new mask line at Kurosaki in [May 2024 for 140 million dollars](https://www.oled-info.com/dnp-starts-producing-8-gen-fmm-masks-its-new-140-million-kuosaki-plant).
