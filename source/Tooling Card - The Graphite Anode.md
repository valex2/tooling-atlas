*Manufacture · Heilongjiang, China · 2010s*
**China's anode-material makers** · round and acid-purify graphite to 99.95%

**Front.** The negative end of a lithium battery is graphite, and the cell is fussy about which graphite. Raw flake mined from the ground is flat and full of grit, and a flat flake packs badly and traps lithium poorly. So Chinese plants take the flake and grind it in a stream of air until each grain tumbles into a tiny rounded ball, then soak the powder in [hydrofluoric acid to dissolve away the rock and metals](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-666X/15/7/827) until the carbon is [99.95 percent pure or better](https://modeldiplomat.com/learn/glossary/graphite-refining). For the man-made kind they bake petroleum coke in electric furnaces at [2,800 to 3,000 degrees Celsius for days](https://www.azom.com/article.aspx?ArticleID=14321) until the carbon rearranges into clean graphite sheets. What is new is not the graphite. It is the recipe and the yield: turning cheap dirty flake into a rounded, ultra-pure, acid-washed ball that a battery line will accept, a step done at scale almost nowhere else.

**Back.** China mines about [79 percent](https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=65305) of the world's natural graphite, but the lock is downstream: it refines [more than 90 percent](https://www.csis.org/analysis/chinas-new-graphite-restrictions) of all graphite and makes [over 90 percent](https://www.iea.org/reports/global-critical-minerals-outlook-2025/executive-summary) of the world's battery anode material, with roughly [90 percent](https://discoveryalert.com.au/chinas-critical-minerals-dominance-processing-control-2025/) of spherical-graphite capacity. South Korea draws about [93 percent](https://www.csis.org/analysis/chinas-new-graphite-restrictions) of its anode material from China and Japan about 90 percent of its graphite, so when Beijing put graphite under export permits [from 1 December 2023](https://www.iea.org/policies/17933-announcement-on-the-optimisation-and-adjustment-of-temporary-export-control-measures-for-graphite-items), every carmaker outside China felt the squeeze, and Washington was [refused permits](https://www.metal.com/en/newscontent/103269594) when Korea and Germany got theirs. US firms like Syrah and Novonix are now building anode plants, but they fight Chinese power that costs a fraction as much and yields tuned over a decade. The mineral is everywhere; the plant that turns it into anode is not, and so far the lead has stayed where the plants are.

*Curator's note:*
Placement: Manufacture cell, Goal Both, Pace-layers, Direct confidence. The materials-processing chokepoint that feeds the battery; sits on the Batteries thread and the Critical Materials thread.
Analysis: This is the deck's "the moat is the processing, not the mineral" case. Graphite is common and the chemistry is public, but the value lives in two boring steps (rounding plus HF purification, and high-temperature graphitization of coke) that need permitted acid plants, cheap power, and a cadre that has tuned yield for years. It pairs against the lithium-cell and gigafactory-line cards on the Batteries thread: the West invented and commercialized the cell, China owns the inputs and the lines. Contrasts with a tacit-craft moat like fermentation: here the moat is more rebuildable, closer to a process anyone could copy if they ate the cost and permitting.
Nuance: The flake belt and several processors sit in Heilongjiang (Jixi/Hegang), but synthetic graphitization concentrates in Inner Mongolia and Sichuan for cheap power, and spherical-graphite plants are spread across China, so "Jixi" is a representative pin, not a single site. Share figures vary by source and year (USGS, IEA, Benchmark, EIA): ~79% mining, >90% refining, ~90%+ anode and spherical, the import-dependence numbers for Korea and Japan are CSIS. The export controls (announced October 2023, effective 1 December 2023) were tightened against the US in December 2024; the regime is permits, not a full ban. The moat is durable on a 3-5 year horizon but rebuildable: HF-free thermal and electrochemical purification routes are advancing, which is why this is Pace-layers, not a permanent Convergence lock.
