*Manufacture · Heilongjiang, China · 2010s*
**China's anode-material makers** · round and acid-purify graphite to 99.95%

**Front.** The negative end of a lithium battery is graphite, and the cell is fussy about which graphite. Raw flake mined from the ground is flat and full of grit, and a flat flake packs badly and traps lithium poorly. So Chinese plants take the flake and grind it in a stream of air until each grain tumbles into a tiny rounded ball, then soak the powder in [hydrofluoric acid to dissolve away the rock and metals](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-666X/15/7/827) until the carbon is [99.95 percent pure or better](https://modeldiplomat.com/learn/glossary/graphite-refining). For the man-made kind they bake petroleum coke in electric furnaces at [2,800 to 3,000 degrees Celsius for days](https://www.azom.com/article.aspx?ArticleID=14321) until the carbon rearranges into clean graphite sheets. What is new is not the graphite. It is the recipe and the yield: turning cheap dirty flake into a rounded, ultra-pure, acid-washed ball that a battery line will accept, a step done at scale almost nowhere else.

**Back.** China mines about [79 percent](https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=65305) of the world's natural graphite, but the lock is downstream: it refines [more than 90 percent](https://www.csis.org/analysis/chinas-new-graphite-restrictions) of all graphite and makes [over 90 percent](https://www.iea.org/reports/global-critical-minerals-outlook-2025/executive-summary) of the world's battery anode material, with roughly [90 percent](https://discoveryalert.com.au/chinas-critical-minerals-dominance-processing-control-2025/) of spherical-graphite capacity. South Korea draws about [93 percent](https://www.csis.org/analysis/chinas-new-graphite-restrictions) of its anode material from China and Japan about 90 percent of its graphite, so when Beijing put graphite under export permits [from 1 December 2023](https://www.iea.org/policies/17933-announcement-on-the-optimisation-and-adjustment-of-temporary-export-control-measures-for-graphite-items), every carmaker outside China felt the squeeze, and Washington was [refused permits](https://www.metal.com/en/newscontent/103269594) when Korea and Germany got theirs. US firms like Syrah and Novonix are now building anode plants, but they fight Chinese power that costs a fraction as much and yields tuned over a decade. The mineral is everywhere; the plant that turns it into anode is not, and the lead has stayed where the plants are.
