*Manufacture · Lyon, France · 1800s*
**Joseph-Marie Jacquard** · punched cards drive a loom

**Front.** In [1804](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Joseph-Marie-Jacquard), a weaver in Lyon named [Joseph-Marie Jacquard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Marie_Jacquard) set out to rid the silk loom of its second worker. Patterned silk needed a boy perched on the loom, pulling cords one at a time to lift the right threads before each pass of the shuttle; Jacquard had [been that boy](https://www.encyclopedia.com/people/science-and-technology/technology-biographies/joseph-marie-jacquard) as a child, and a good day's weaving came to about [one inch](https://www.thehenryford.org/collections/explore/articles/sparking-innovation-the-jacquard-loom) of cloth. He punched the pattern into stiff cards as rows of holes and [laced the cards into a chain](https://www.computerhistory.org/storageengine/punched-cards-control-jacquard-loom/) that fed over a bed of spring needles on top of the loom; where a hole let a needle through, the needle's hook lifted that thread for the pass. The pattern now lived in the cards, so to weave a different silk the weaver hung a different chain and touched nothing else.

**Back.** Lyon's silk weavers [burned the new looms](https://www.encyclopedia.com/people/science-and-technology/technology-biographies/joseph-marie-jacquard) and attacked Jacquard, sure the cards would take their bread; Napoleon's government [declared the loom public property](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Marie_Jacquard) and paid him 3,000 francs a year plus 50 francs for every loom sold. By [1812](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Joseph-Marie-Jacquard) some 11,000 were weaving in France, and the loom crossed to England [by 1820](https://www.thehenryford.org/collections/explore/articles/sparking-innovation-the-jacquard-loom). [Charles Babbage](https://www.computerhistory.org/storageengine/punched-cards-control-jacquard-loom/) kept a silk portrait of Jacquard woven from [24,000 cards](https://www.computerhistory.org/storageengine/punched-cards-control-jacquard-loom/) and chose the same cards to feed his Analytical Engine; in 1890 [Herman Hollerith](https://www.computerhistory.org/storageengine/punched-cards-control-jacquard-loom/) punched them to count the American census. Lyon raised a [statue of Jacquard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Marie_Jacquard) in 1840; Hollerith's card company became IBM.
