*Make · Cologne, Germany · 1870s*
**Nicolaus Otto** · four-stroke compressed-charge gas engine

**Front.** In 1876, [Nicolaus Otto](https://www.invent.org/inductees/nicolaus-august-otto), a grocer's apprentice who had spent his twenties selling tea and sugar across western Germany, ran an engine factory at [Deutz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deutz_AG), across the river from Cologne. The engine his factory sold was a tower: each firing hurled a free piston up a toothed rack with a bang, and no amount of tinkering could push it much past [three horsepower](https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/history/ottos-practical-internal-combustion-engine). That year Otto and his workshop built a low machine that squeezed the coal-gas and air tight inside the cylinder before the flame reached them, giving the piston four strokes for every push: draw in, squeeze, burn, sweep out. Every gas engine before had burned its charge loose at street pressure; packing it first roughly [tripled](https://www.encyclopedia.com/science/encyclopedias-almanacs-transcripts-and-maps/internal-combustion-engine-0) the work a cylinder pulled from its fuel, and did it so smoothly the trade called the new machine the Silent Otto.

**Back.** Otto's 1877 patent [fell in 1886](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolaus_Otto), when an 1862 paper by the Frenchman [Alphonse Beau de Rochas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphonse_Beau_de_Rochas) describing the same four strokes surfaced in court, and after that anyone could build the cycle. Otto's own men moved first: [Gottlieb Daimler and Wilhelm Maybach](https://group.mercedes-benz.com/company/tradition/company-history/1885-1886.html), who had run the Deutz shop floor, left in 1882 to shrink the engine and feed it petrol, and by 1886 Carl Benz had one rolling under a three-wheeled carriage. More than [30,000](https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/history/ottos-practical-internal-combustion-engine) Otto engines sold within ten years, and at Augsburg in 1897 [Rudolf Diesel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudolf_Diesel) pushed the same squeeze far past anything Otto's flame could stand. Nearly every petrol car built since has kept the four beats Otto set in Deutz.
