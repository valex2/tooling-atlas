*Manufacture · Portsmouth, UK · 1800s*
**Marc Isambard Brunel & Henry Maudslay** · a 45-machine block-making line

**Front.** In 1801, [Marc Isambard Brunel](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Dictionary_of_National_Biography,_1885-1900/Brunel,_Marc_Isambard), a French engineer who had fled the Revolution and washed up in London, patented a way to cut ships' pulley blocks by machine. The Royal Navy was the customer to have: each big warship ran on about [a thousand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portsmouth_Block_Mills) of these wooden blocks, the fleet wore through over 100,000 a year, and every one was shaped by hand. Brunel drew a chain of machines that would each make one cut, and [Henry Maudslay](https://www.gracesguide.co.uk/Portsmouth_Block_Mills) built all [45 of them](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portsmouth_Block_Mills) in iron in his London shop; from [January 1803](https://www.gracesguide.co.uk/Portsmouth_Block_Mills) they stood in rows at Portsmouth dockyard, driven by steam, and an elm log passed from machine to machine until it fell out the end as a finished block. Each machine held one piece of the blockmaker's craft, so the men who fed the line needed almost none of their own.

**Back.** The line took years to tune, but by [1808](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portsmouth_Block_Mills) it was cutting 130,000 blocks a year, ten men doing the work of [110 skilled blockmakers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portsmouth_Block_Mills) and saving the Navy about [£24,000](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Dictionary_of_National_Biography,_1885-1900/Brunel,_Marc_Isambard) in the first full year. [Nelson toured the machines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMNB_Portsmouth) before boarding HMS Victory for Trafalgar, and some of them were still cutting blocks in the same building in the [1960s](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portsmouth_Block_Mills). But Britain left the idea in the dockyard: factories built from single-purpose machines spread into general manufacture only [a century later](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portsmouth_Block_Mills), and the method arrived from America.
