*Manufacture · Uppsala, Sweden · 1970s*
**Helena Hjelm and John Sjöquist** · beads that catch antibodies by the tail

**Front.** John Sjöquist had spent ten years on a sticky protein sitting on the skin of *Staphylococcus aureus*. He and a colleague had [shown in 1962 that it was a protein and not a sugar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein_A), and by then everyone knew it grabbed antibodies. In November 1972 he and Helena and Kerstin Hjelm [glued that protein onto porous agarose beads and packed them into a column](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/4630462/), using a bead and a coupling trick that [Uppsala chemists had worked out five years earlier](https://www.nature.com/articles/2141302a0). Then they poured serum through it. Antibodies stopped in the beads and everything else ran out the bottom, and a wash of acid let the antibodies go again. The protein grips the blunt tail end that every antibody of a class shares, so the column catches the molecule without caring what it was built to fight.

**Back.** Pharmacia sold the first [protein A resin in 1978](https://aiche.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/btpr.2324); it held five grams of antibody per litre of beads and cleared about 0.7 grams an hour. Ortho's Orthoclone, [the first antibody drug, approved in 1986](https://aiche.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/btpr.2324), was captured on one. Capacity has climbed about 5.5 percent a year ever since, so a 2015 resin holds sixty grams per litre and clears nineteen an hour, and [86 percent of antibody drugs filed with the FDA](https://aiche.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/btpr.2324) go through the step. Nobody owns the idea, which is printed in a fifty-year-old paper, and everybody has to buy the beads: they run [roughly 5,500 to 16,000 dollars a litre](https://www.bioprocessintl.com/chromatography/cost-estimation-for-protein-a-chromatography-an-in-silico-approach-to-mab-purification-strategy), the priciest thing a drug plant consumes, and [Cytiva still bakes most of the world's supply in Uppsala](https://www.cytivalifesciences.com/en/us/news-center/cytiva-advances-resins-portfolio-10001), a mile or so from where the Hjelms packed the first one.
