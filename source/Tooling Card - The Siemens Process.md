*Manufacture · Germany · 1950s*
**Siemens** · silicon grown from gas on a hot rod

**Front.** In [1954](https://patents.google.com/patent/DE1102117B/en), chemists at a Siemens lab in the Franconian village of Pretzfeld wanted silicon clean enough to build the new transistors and power rectifiers. You could sweep a molten band along a silicon bar to push the dirt to one end, but you first needed a bar that was already very pure, and pulling silicon that clean out of ore was the wall. So [Friedrich Bischoff](https://patents.google.com/patent/DE1102117B/en) ran a silicon-carrying gas, trichlorosilane mixed with hydrogen, past a thin silicon thread heated by an electric current to about [1,150 °C](https://www.bernreuter.com/polysilicon/production-processes/); on the glowing thread the gas split apart and pure silicon plated out, layer on layer, until the thread grew into a fat rod. He had grown a clean solid up out of a gas instead of cleaning a dirty one.

**Back.** Those fat rods became the raw silicon for almost every chip and solar cell that came after. Three years earlier, [Bell Labs had learned to sweep a molten zone along a silicon bar](https://www.computerhistory.org/siliconengine/development-of-zone-refining/) to drive the dirt to one end, but that trick needed a clean bar to start with, and the Siemens process is what grew it. Wacker in Germany scaled the recipe up, then American and Japanese makers copied it, and it kept spreading until it left home: in 2024 Chinese firms poured [93.5% of the world's polysilicon](https://taiyangnews.info/business/china-dominates-2024-global-polysilicon-production), and [Wacker is the only Western maker](https://www.renewable-energy-industry.com/news/world/article-7121-nine-of-the-ten-largest-polysilicon-manufacturers-come-from-china-wacker-remains-the-only-western-polysilicon-producer) still among the ten largest.
