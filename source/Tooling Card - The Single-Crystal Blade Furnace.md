*Manufacture · East Hartford, Connecticut, USA · 1980s*
**Frank VerSnyder and the Pratt & Whitney foundry team** · a furnace that freezes a blade as one crystal

**Front.** In September 1982 a Boeing 767 took off on Pratt & Whitney [JT9D-7R4 engines](https://www.asme.org/about-asme/engineering-history/landmarks/265-pratt-amp-whitney-single-crystal-turbine), the first airliner to fly on a new kind of turbine blade. The blade's problem was creep: hot, heavily loaded metal slowly stretches and tears along the seams where one crystal grain meets the next. The fix, worked out over two decades at the company's research lab by a team under [Frank VerSnyder](https://www.machinedesign.com/mechanical-motion-systems/article/21836518/single-crystal-turbine-blades-earn-asme-milestone-status), removed the seams. Their furnace sat the filled mold on a water-cooled copper plate so freezing started at the base, then drew the mold slowly down out of the heat at a few inches an hour, so the freezing front climbed the blade instead of starting everywhere at once. Just above the plate the mold narrowed into a tight corkscrew passage, so cramped that only [one growing crystal could snake through](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Single_crystal) and go on to fill the blade. The blade came out as a single grain of metal with no boundaries to tear, cast to shape.

**Back.** A single-crystal blade lasts [roughly nine times longer](https://www.machinedesign.com/mechanical-motion-systems/article/21836518/single-crystal-turbine-blades-earn-asme-milestone-status) under creep than ordinary cast metal, so the engine could run hotter and burn less fuel. The withdrawal rate, the temperature gradient across the freezing front, and the shape of the selector are set by trial in each foundry, and no blueprint carries them. Every Western engine maker took up the method, and [Precision Castparts and Howmet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Howmet_Aerospace) still pour most of the world's hot-section blades between them. China [needed about 35 years](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xian_WS-9_Qinling) just to copy an older engine.
