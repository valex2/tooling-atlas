*Measure · Wotton-under-Edge, UK · 1970s*
**David McMurtry (Renishaw)** · a stylus that signals touch

**Front.** One day in [1972](https://www.renishaw.com/en/our-history--32458), a young design engineer at Rolls-Royce in Bristol named [David McMurtry](https://www.ingenia.org.uk/articles/sir-david-mcmurtry-freng-frs-turning-patents-into-profits/) was stuck on the [Olympus engines](https://www.renishaw.com/en/tribute-to-sir-david-mcmurtry--49562) being built for Concorde. The engines were dressed in thin fuel pipes, some just a [quarter of an inch](https://www.fundinguniverse.com/company-histories/renishaw-plc-history/) across, and every measuring tool of the day worked by pressing, so it bent the pipe it was sent to measure. Over a weekend at home he built a probe out of [six ball bearings and a piece of his bedroom carpet](https://www.fundinguniverse.com/company-histories/renishaw-plc-history/): a stylus standing on three feet, each foot seated on the balls, the whole stand holding one electric circuit closed, so the lightest graze on a pipe tipped a foot off its seat and cut the signal. The machine carrying the probe logged where its arm stood the instant the signal cut; the touch was too light to bend anything, and it fired at the same spot every time.

**Back.** Rolls-Royce [filed the patent](https://patents.google.com/patent/GB1445977A/en) that September but had no wish to sell instruments, so in 1973 McMurtry and a fellow engineer, [John Deer](https://www.renishaw.com/en/tribute-to-sir-david-mcmurtry--49562), licensed the probe back and registered [Renishaw](https://www.renishaw.com/en/our-history--32458), soldering their first [ten probes](https://www.tctmagazine.com/40-years-of-renishaw/) in Deer's house for a measuring-machine maker. The maker showed the probe at a trade fair, rival machine builders lined up, and by 1976 the pair were making probes in a former [ice cream factory](https://www.renishaw.com/en/our-history--32458) in Wotton-under-Edge. The lead compounded from there: Renishaw took as much as [80 percent](https://www.fundinguniverse.com/company-histories/renishaw-plc-history/) of the world market for probes and never gave it back, and it still runs from the same Gloucestershire town, now [5,000 people in 36 countries](https://www.renishaw.com/en/tribute-to-sir-david-mcmurtry--49562).
