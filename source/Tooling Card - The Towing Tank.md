*Measure · Torquay, UK · 1870s*
**William Froude** · trough for towing model ships

**Front.** In 1870, [William Froude](https://www.gracesguide.co.uk/William_Froude), a railway engineer who had quit the profession years before to care for his sick father in Devon, talked the Admiralty into an odd purchase: [£2,000](http://www.navweaps.com/index_tech/tech-010.php) for a shed full of water behind his house at Torquay. No one could say how much engine a new ironclad needed until the ship was already built, and most engineers held that small models were no guide, because drag refuses to scale in any simple way. Froude dug a roofed trough [250 feet long](https://www.gracesguide.co.uk/Admiralty_Experiment_Works), cast model hulls in [paraffin wax](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/engineering/ship-model-tanks), hung each one from a carriage on rails above the water, and towed it while a balance read how hard the water pulled back. Tow the model at a speed set by the [square root of its length](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/engineering/froude-similarity), take away the skin friction he had measured by [towing thin planks](https://www.encyclopedia.com/people/science-and-technology/technology-biographies/william-froude), and the drag left over scaled straight up to the ship: a warship's power bill, read off a twelve-foot wax toy before any iron was cut.

**Back.** The Navy put the rule to sea, towing the real [Greyhound](https://www.usni.org/magazines/proceedings/1883/january/towing-experiments-models-determine-resistance-full-sized-ships), a warship of about 1,160 tons, behind another ship on a 48-foot spar, and in [1874](https://www.encyclopedia.com/people/science-and-technology/technology-biographies/william-froude) Froude reported that the full-size drag came out close to what his wax model had said. Other yards copied the trough: the Dutch built one at Amsterdam, and the shipbuilder [Denny](https://www.nationaltransporttrust.org.uk/heritage-sites/heritage-detail/denny-tank) at Dumbarton raised the first private tank in 1883. By [1933](https://ittc.info/media/1445/opening-address.pdf) the keepers of the world's tanks were meeting as their own congress, ten countries at the first sitting, and a new hull still goes down a trough as a model first, towed at a speed set by the number that carries his name, the [Froude number](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/engineering/froude-similarity).
