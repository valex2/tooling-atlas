*Make · Berkeley, England · 1790s*
**Edward Jenner** · scratching a mild germ in to train the body

**Front.** In May [1796](https://www.history.com/this-day-in-history/may-14/jenner-tests-smallpox-vaccine) Edward Jenner was a country doctor in Berkeley, Gloucestershire, watching smallpox kill and disfigure his neighbors. He had noticed that milkmaids who caught cowpox, a mild sore from cattle, seemed to never get smallpox. He took fluid from a cowpox blister on the milkmaid [Sarah Nelmes](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11463195/) and scratched it into the arm of an eight-year-old boy, James Phipps; the boy got one small sore and recovered. The new thing was the next step: six weeks later Jenner scratched real [smallpox](https://www.who.int/news-room/spotlight/history-of-vaccination/a-brief-history-of-vaccination) into the boy and no disease came, proving you could give the body a safe practice fight against a killer.

**Back.** Jenner named the method after the cow, *vacca*, and doctors across Europe copied the scratch within a few years. The deeper move came eighty years later: [Louis Pasteur](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Louis-Pasteur/Vaccine-development) found he could weaken a germ in the lab, first chicken cholera in [1879](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3342039/), then anthrax, then rabies in 1885, so a vaccine no longer needed a lucky cousin disease like cowpox. That step, called attenuation, turned Jenner's one trick into a general recipe, and the lead in training the immune system compounded from there into the whole century of vaccines that followed.

(2) Analysis: Builds on Leeuwenhoek's microscope, which first showed the living germ world this method works against. Enables the later medicine line up to Monoclonal Antibodies, where immunity is engineered rather than coaxed. Fits the larger case as a tool whose lead clearly migrated, from Jenner's single empirical trick to Pasteur's repeatable laboratory method.
(3) Nuance: Jenner was not strictly first to use cowpox; Benjamin Jesty inoculated his family in 1774, but Jenner did the test and the publication that made it a method others adopted. Variolation, deliberate mild smallpox, predates him in Asia and the Ottoman world, but it used the live killer and could spread the disease, which is the danger cowpox removed. The Sarah Nelmes and James Phipps names are well documented; the exact second-inoculation date (commonly July 1) varies slightly across sources.
