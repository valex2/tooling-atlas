*Manufacture · Oberkochen, Germany · 2010s*
**Carl Zeiss SMT** · mirror columns for chip printers

**Front.** In 2017, in Oberkochen, a small town in the hills of southern Germany, opticians at [Carl Zeiss](https://www.zeiss.com/semiconductor-manufacturing-technology/inspiring-technology/euv-lithography.html) were finishing mirrors for a [chip-printing machine](https://cdrinfo.com/d7/content/asml-3400b-production-platform-euvl-ready-shipping) being put together in Holland. The machine printed with light of such short waves that glass soaks them up, so the light had to bounce from mirror to mirror, and a bump a few atoms tall would smear the print. The Zeiss crew ground each mirror and read its surface with light waves; where a spot stood a few atoms too high, they fired a thin beam of charged atoms to shave it down, then measured again, around and around for [two to three years](https://www.electrooptics.com/feature/mirror-semicon) per mirror. Scale a finished mirror up to the size of Germany and its tallest bump would rise [a tenth of a millimeter](https://www.zeiss.com/semiconductor-manufacturing-technology/smt-magazine/so-does-euv-lithography-work.html).

**Back.** By [2019](https://bits-chips.com/article/tsmc-starts-volume-production-of-euv-chips/) TSMC in Taiwan was printing chips with the new machines by the million, and a chip the size of a fingernail now carries [57 billion transistors](https://www.zeiss.com/semiconductor-manufacturing-technology/news-and-events/smt-press-releases/2025/euv-30-years.html). ASML, the only company that builds the machines, can buy the mirrors nowhere else, so it [paid €1 billion for a 24.9 percent share](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2016/zeiss-and-asml-strengthen-partnership-for-next-generation-of-euv-lithography) of the Zeiss unit and pledged €760 million more so the still bigger mirrors of the next machine would get built. Nikon and Canon, whose lenses had ruled chip printing in the 1980s, both dropped out of the mirror race; in 2020 Germany's president gave the Zeiss polishers [the country's top technology prize](https://www.iof.fraunhofer.de/en/pressrelease/2020/deutscher-zukunftspreis-2020.html), and every advanced chip on earth still passes under mirrors finished in the one town.
